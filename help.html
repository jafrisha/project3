WINTER VOLUNTEERS

Serving Middlesex County, New Jersey

Help Section for Volunteer Members -- You must Read & Agree before Signing Up!

1) How does the volunteering program work?

The program is focused on helping disabled and senior residents of the local community who require assistance clearing snow, which may include stairways, sidewalks, and driveways. Some may request to have their cars cleared of snow.

Throughout the winter season, the administrators of this website will actively monitor weather forecasts for any potentially hazardous wintry precipitation. As a rough guideline, when at least 2 inches of snow accumulations are expected, volunteers will receive notifications by email and text message alerting them to begin preparations for the weather. Once snowfall starts, volunteers electronically receive the address(es) where they are needed. Volunteers should arrive within 12 to 24 hours after snowfall has ended.

2) How far will volunteers need to travel?

Please be prepared to travel up to 20 miles one-way. Volunteers are randomly assigned to addresses within the same county, sometimes near one another, sometimes farther apart. Volunteers must take every precaution necessary for traveling in winter weather, and only travel as long as there no restrictions placed on motorists. Volunteers are expected to have access to safe, reliable vehicles in order to participate in the program.

3) What supplies are needed?

Volunteers must arrive with their own supplies and tools, but are free to use plastic snow shovels, snow blowers, or other tools of their choice. Care must be exercised to avoid damaging property while clearing snow by any given means. Once snow is cleared, a light coating of rock salt should be sprinkled on surfaces to avoid developing icy spots.

4) How much time should volunteers devote?

The maximum time a volunteer should spend at one address is 2 hours. Volunteers will already have been provided with a set of instructions written by the resident that will direct them where to clean. Upon arrival, the resident may also communicate any additional instructions. Volunteers can sign up to complete either one or two jobs in a single day, and can also choose to be called on 2 or more consecutive days, depending on the amount of snowfall received.

5) Will volunteers receive training?

All new volunteers will participate in a brief orientation via a one-on-one video call. Additionally, volunteers will receive training by viewing a specially prepared 15-minute online video presenation.

Help Section for Disabled/Senior Citizen Members -- You must Read & Agree before Signing Up!

1) Who is eligible to sign up for this service?

The program is available free-of-charge to all disabled and senior residents of New Jersey. Seniors must provide proof of age in the form of a valid, government issued photo ID. Disabled persons may register via this website, and will receive a cerification form by regular mail, which must be completed, signed by an approved medical pracitioner, and mailed back.

2) How will the volunteers know what areas need cleaning?

During registration, residents must specify what areas should be cleaned. Volunteers are instructed to remain outdoors and should not be asked to enter the residence. However, it is possible to communicate any additional instructions to volunteers when they arrive.

3) What supplies are needed?

Volunteers come prepared with all of their own supplies and tools, but residents may choose to make additional tools available to be used at a volunteer's own discretion. Please be advised that volunteers are instructed to sprinkle a layer of rock salt on cleaned surfaces.

4) How much time will a volunteer spend?

The maximum time a volunteers should spend at one address is 2 hours. Volunteers will already have been provided with a set of instructions written by the resident that will direct them where to clean. Upon arrival, residents may also communicate any additional instructions.
